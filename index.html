ABSTRACT

Geoff Brumfiel is a veteran science and technology journalist with 25 years of experience in the field. He's reported extensively on space exploration, nuclear weapons, national security, artificial intelligence and more.

Geoff holds a double-degree in physics and English. He spent over a decade reporting on fundamental physics and science policy for Nature magazine (reporting from both the London and Washington bureaus). He joined NPR in 2013 and has travelled to Fukushima, where he reported on the aftermath of the 2011 nuclear disaster, and deep inside the tunnels where America tests its nuclear weapons.

1.2 DETAILED EMPLOYMENT NOTES

NPR — Senior Editor & Correspondent (Oct 2019 – Present). Covers nuclear weapons policy, space and national security, and AI-driven disinformation for broadcast and digital platforms. Produces enterprise investigations, breaking news reports, and explanatory features reaching NPR's weekly audience. Develops and edits science coverage across the NPR newsroom.

NPR — Deputy Sr. Supervising Editor, Science (Mar 2016 – Oct 2019). Directed daily science and health coverage across all NPR platforms, supervising a team of correspondents, editors, and producers. Managed coverage of major science stories including climate, public health, and technology policy.

NPR — Science Correspondent (Mar 2013 – Mar 2016). On-air correspondent covering science, technology, and national security for NPR newscasts and programs. Reported from the field on events including space launches, nuclear policy developments, and emerging technology.

Nature Magazine — Senior Reporter (Aug 2007 – Mar 2013). Covered physical sciences, defense research, and science policy from Nature's London bureau for the world's leading multidisciplinary science journal. Reported on major developments including the Higgs boson discovery, Fukushima nuclear disaster, and European science policy.

Nature Magazine — Washington Correspondent (Jan 2002 – Aug 2007). Covered U.S. science policy, federal research funding, and physics for Nature from Washington, D.C. Reported on the post-9/11 science security landscape, NASA programs, and Department of Energy research priorities.

APS / Physical Review Focus — Reporter & Asst. to the Editor (Aug 2001 – Dec 2001). Wrote accessible summaries of physics research published in Physical Review journals for a general scientific audience.
